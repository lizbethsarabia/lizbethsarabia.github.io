Some researchers frame free will not as a metaphysical absolute, but as a perception. A psychological experience consists of two main components:

  • Sense of willing - the feeling that oneself initiates or causes an action.
  • Sense of self-agency - the sense that oneself is the only one who controls or authorizes the action.
